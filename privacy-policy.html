1. What personal data we collect

We may collect the following personal data:

  • your name, email address, phone number, job title, company and country;
  • information you send us through contact forms, email, speaker nomination forms or event-related forms;
  • event registration details, including ticket type, billing details and attendance preferences;
  • photos, videos and recordings from our events;
  • basic technical information about your visit to this website, such as browser type, device, pages visited and approximate location.

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data through this website. Please do not send us sensitive information unless it is necessary for a specific request.

2. How we use your data

We use personal data to:

  • respond to your messages and enquiries;
  • manage event registrations, tickets, attendance, invoices and practical event communication;
  • send updates about events you registered for or asked about;
  • manage speakers, sponsors, partners and event participants;
  • improve our website, events and communication;
  • promote our events, including by using selected photos, videos and event materials;
  • comply with legal, tax and accounting obligations.

We process personal data only where we have a valid reason to do so. This may be because we need it to provide a service, because we have a legal obligation, because you gave us consent, or because we have a legitimate interest in running and promoting our events.

3. Event photos, videos and recordings

We may take photos, videos and recordings at our events. These may be used on our website, social media, newsletters, presentations and other promotional materials.

We try to use event media in a respectful and reasonable way. If you have a concern about a photo or video in which you appear, contact us at maria@businessofcompetition.eu and we will review it.

4. Cookies and analytics

This website may use cookies and similar technologies to make the site work properly and to understand how visitors use it.

Essential cookies may be used without your consent because they are needed for the website to function. Analytics or marketing cookies, if used, will only be used where allowed by law and, where required, with your consent.

You can usually control cookies through your browser settings.

5. Third-party services

We may use third-party service providers to run this website, manage event registrations, process payments, send emails, analyse website traffic, host forms, manage customer relationships, or support event operations.

These providers may process personal data on our behalf. We only share the data that is necessary for the relevant service.

If event tickets or payments are handled through a third-party platform, that platform may also process your data under its own privacy policy.

6. How long we keep your data

We keep personal data only for as long as reasonably necessary.

For example:

  • enquiry data is kept for as long as needed to respond and follow up;
  • event registration and billing data may be kept for legal, tax and accounting purposes;
  • marketing contact data is kept until you unsubscribe or ask us to delete it;
  • event photos and materials may be kept as part of our event archive and promotional materials.

7. Your rights

Depending on the situation, you may have the right to:

  • ask what personal data we hold about you;
  • ask us to correct inaccurate data;
  • ask us to delete your data;
  • ask us to restrict how we use your data;
  • object to certain uses of your data;
  • withdraw consent where we rely on consent;
  • ask for a copy of your data in a portable format.

To exercise your rights, contact us at maria@businessofcompetition.eu.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection authority. In the Czech Republic, this is the Office for Personal Data Protection, Úřad pro ochranu osobních údajů.

8. Data security

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal data. No website or online service is completely secure, but we try to handle personal data responsibly and limit access to people and providers who need it.

9. International transfers

Some of the tools and service providers we use may process data outside your country or outside the European Economic Area. Where this happens, we aim to use appropriate safeguards required by applicable data protection law.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The latest version will always be available on this page.
